# Synthetic data policy

The version 1.0 release is self-contained and uses only deterministic or
seed-controlled synthetic data. This policy applies to every executable
notebook, example, validation challenge, figure, and tabular result distributed
with the repository.

## Included data classes

1. **Synthetic orientation fields.** Smooth or perturbed nematic directions are
   created analytically on regular grids or finite-element meshes.
2. **Synthetic structural-order fields.** Bounded fields in `[0, 1]` are
   generated from analytic backgrounds, localized defects, and controlled
   perturbations.
3. **Synthetic polarimetry-like fields.** Azimuth and retardance-like maps are
   generated from known latent structure, then perturbed by documented noise
   and validity masks.
4. **Synthetic mechanical observations.** Stress observations are produced by
   the implemented constitutive law under prescribed deformation gradients and
   perturbed by a known Gaussian noise model.
5. **Synthetic blind-validation targets.** Training observations and test inputs
   are separated from hidden test targets to emulate a predictive validation
   workflow.

## Evidence boundary

The release demonstrates that the software is internally consistent and can
recover or predict quantities generated by its documented mathematical model.
It does not demonstrate accuracy for a particular tissue, specimen, disease,
measurement system, or clinical population.

## Regeneration

Every generated file is reproducible from source. Randomized generators expose
an explicit seed, and release-critical files are listed with SHA-256 hashes in
`release_manifest.json`.
